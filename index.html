ABOUT

Muay Thai is a combat sport from Thailand that uses stand-up striking along with various clinching techniques, it's a great way to get fit and improve your cardio!

Muay Thai is referred to as the "Art of Eight Limbs" or the "Science of Eight Limbs" because it makes use of punches, kicks, elbows and knee strikes, thus using eight "points of contact", as opposed to "two points" (fists) in boxing and "four points" (hands and feet) used in other combat sports, such as kickboxing, boxing, and savate.

SESSIONS


We offer four sessions a week and all sessions are included in the membership. You are free to go to the sessions that you think would suit you best!
Non-members will be allowed to join a session for a fee, but due to limited space, priority will be given to members.

The club will provide all the necessary kit for the training and strength and conditioning sessions - just make sure that you are wearing comfortable sports clothes! Handwraps will not be provided as they are not mandatory but they can be purchased externally.

For sparring sessions, the club will provide gloves and shinguards, however you will need to purchase your mouth guard and groin guard (if applicable) yourself.

All our sessions are aimed to be beginner friendly, however we strongly encourage you to go to several training sessions first before going to any sparring sessions and make sure you have the right equipment!

FIGHTS

We have multiple fights held throughout the year, both decision (N-class) and non-decision, interclubs and intraclubs. We also have our annual home show held in the Potterow.

Here are some of the fights throughout the year:

  • Glasgow University Fight Night
  • EUMT Intraclub
  • Edinburgh University Muay Thai Fight Night
  • Stirling University Fight Night
  • Glasgow Caledonian University Fight Night
